Welcome to Binti Kahawa

Our Project Phases

Our projects go through the following phases in order to deliver quality products.

Grouping women

we are currently working with 6,400 women in 32 counties growing coffee in Kenya, in groups of 200 women per county to go in to organic farming in an affordable way in the coffee sector within 4 years. In this project each group of 200 women require to have 250 coffee bushes each farmer.

Target regions

We target the Kenyan regions of Rift Valley, Central, Eastern, Coast Region and Western which are agriculturally active in coffee farming. Through Co-operative Union and Privet Estates, Binti Kahawa will work with 200 women Coffee farmer from each county who will each rehabilitate 250 coffee trees by using organic fertilizer to clean the soli, improve quality and increase coffee production from 3kg per tree to 15kg per tree in 4 years.

Period of production

our main focus will be to work with women who farm coffee. Each farmer will be required to enhance and rehabilitate 250 coffee trees in to organic coffee. Each woman will need 20 sachets of each 20g plant enhancer and soil conditioner fertilizer to spray for a period of a year at a total cost of 8,000/= Kenya shillings for each farmer.

Educating and resource mobilization

Educating and resource mobilization to increase the number of trees produced per year

Our Project Impact

  • ORGANIC CERTIFICATON

    production of quality coffee that has eventually attained organic certification.

  • ELIMINATION OF CHEMICALS IN OUR LOCAL DRINKING WATER

    This initiative shall lower and eventually eliminate chemicals in our local drinking Water which is currently emanated from agricultural practices.

  • CROP INSURANCE

    The farmers can enjoy crop insurance which protects the minimum yield to between 5-7 Kg of cherry per tree to mitigate and lower vulnerabilities caused by climate change and global warming.

  • ECONOMIC SUSTAINABILITY

    This enables women in coffee farming to get their income and afford better living

About Us

Binti Kahawa. and partners are working on women needs and helping to provide tools, training and resources to enable them accomplish their goals sustainably. Where women are engaged, conservation and development goals are more effectively achieved.  Further, the benefits of engaging women not only improve the livelihoods of women themselves, but also offer greater impact for entire communities. When women are empowered, poverty declines, children are healthier, and economies grow stronger. Binti Kahawa will collaborate with privet planters, co-operative unions and affiliated societies in all the coffee growing Counties in the initiative of women in coffee. Working in partnership with our partners, this project aims to increase coffee incomes by 100 percent for privet planters and smallholder women in coffee farming in Kenya.
