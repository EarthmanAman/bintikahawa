About us

Binti Kahawa. and partners are working on women needs and helping to provide tools, training and resources to enable them accomplish their goals sustainably. Where women are engaged, conservation and development goals are more effectively achieved.  Further, the benefits of engaging women not only improve the livelihoods of women themselves, but also offer greater impact for entire communities. When women are empowered, poverty declines, children are healthier, and economies grow stronger. Binti Kahawa will collaborate with privet planters, co-operative unions and affiliated societies in all the coffee growing Counties in the initiative of women in coffee. Working in partnership with our partners, this project aims to increase coffee incomes by 100 percent for privet planters and smallholder women in coffee farming in Kenya.

Vision

The country’s climatic, geographical and soil conditions aid in production of one of the world's best coffee. But sub-optimal farming and processing practices limit the quality and quantity of coffee that small scale women farmers produce. We are working with small scale women coffee farmers to improve their cultivation in order to increase the quality and quantity of their coffee. This Intervention will help thousands of women farmers to increase their coffee yield, double their incomes over the next 4 years. Binti Kahawa recognizes the important role played by women in coffee production since most of the labor in coffee production involves women and yet they do not get commensurate income from the sales. Helping women graduate from extreme poverty with a proven track record, measurable results and a transformative approach to alleviating poverty and building resiliency in the rural Kenya is our main objective.

Mission

Binti Kahawa is passionate to deliver our farmers from current stats to a profitable sustainable coffee farming practices, where farmers are financial able to support themselves and families. We want a concept where farmers produce quality and quantity coffee where coffee enthusiast will for the first time identify where his/her coffee comes from, who is the farmer and how it was processed and when.

Our story

Since Lending institutions target women groups but their interests rates are too expensive for small scale coffee farmer.
